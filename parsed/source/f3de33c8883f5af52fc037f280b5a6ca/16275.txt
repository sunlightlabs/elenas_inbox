Passed (94-4) S. 1005- Department of Defense FY98 Appropriations.
        [SAP sent 7/14: Admin. has serious concerns]

While under consideration, the Senate took the following action on S. 1005:
o       Rejected (15-83) Harkin amendment which would have prohibited the
use of DoD funds for restructuring costs involving defense company mergers.
o       Adopted (voice vote) Stevens amendment to require the Secretary


of Defense to report by October 1, 1997 detailing the U.S. costs resulting
from the expansion of NATO for the Czech Republic, Poland, and Hungary for
FY 1998-2002 and the costs of any new commitments or obligations
associated with the admittance of new members to NATO.

Began consideration & took the following action on S. 1004 - Energy and
Water FY 1998 Appropriations bill.
 [SAP sent 7/15: Admin. strongly objects to reallocation of national
defense funds from DoE programs to DoD programs and objects to large
increases in Corps water projects]
o        Tabled (56-42) Feingold amendment which would have required the
Interior Secretary to submit a report to Congress by Nov. 15, 1997
detailing ways to reduce the cost of the Animas-La Plata irrigation
project in Southwestern Colorado.

HOUSE
Passed (238-192) H.R. 2107 - Interior Appropriations Act for FY 1998
[Floor SAP sent 7/10: Senior advisers veto recommendation over lack of
funding for NEA]

While under consideration, the House took the following ·action on H.R.
2107:
o       Rejected (96-328) Chabot amendment to eliminate funding for the
National Endowment for the Humanities.
o       Passed (314-109) Crapo amendment to establish a "Deficit
Reduction Lockbox" to ensure that spending reductions made are used for
deficit reduction and not to fund other programs.
o       Rejected (208-216) Istook amendment to prohibit the use of funds
in the bill by the Bureau of Indian Affairs to transfer any new land into
trust unless a binding agreement is reached between Indian tribes, states,
and local governments regarding state & local excise taxes on retail sales
to non-Indians on the new trust land.
o       Passed (222-203) Coburn amendment to prohibit use of any of the
funds in the bill for the U.S. Man and the Biosphere Program or the World
Heritage Program administered by the United Nations Educational,
Scientific, and Cultural Organization.
o       Passed (396-25) Weldon amendment to prohibit the NPS from using
funds to designate any portion of Canaveral National Seashore in Florida
as a "clothing optional" area (or "nude beach"), if the designation would
be contrary to local county ordinance.

Began consideration of H.R. 2158 - VA/HUD Appropriations Act for FY 1998
[Floor SAP sent 7/15: Senior advisers veto threat over amendment to
terminate Americorps, shifting $50 million to study Gulf War Syndrome and
use the balance for deficit reduction]
