CONGRESS TODAY (7/9)
SENATE
Continued consideration of S. 936 - National Defense Authorization Act for
Fiscal Year 1998. Final vote possible by the end of the week.
        [SAP sent 6/19: Senior Advisors Recommend Veto)



While under consideration, the Senate took the following action on S. 936
- National Defense Authorization Act for FY 1998:
o        Adopted (66-33) Dorgan amendment to a McCain amendment that
requires the Defense Secretary to report to Congress on the cost
effectiveness of past base closures and to make recommendations on the
need for future rounds of base closure. The amendment also strikes the
underlining authority for new base closures. Subsequently, the McCain
amendment, as amended, was passed by voice vote.
o        Adopted (98-0) Dodd amendment to express the sense of Congress
that the July 6, 1997 elections in Mexico were free, fair & impartial.
o        Rejected (46-53) Gorton amendment which would have required the
Secretary to re-open the selection process for the U.S.S. Missouri.
o        Adopted (65-33) Thurmond motion to table the Wellstone amendment
to require the Defense Secretary to transfer $5 million to the Agriculture
Secretary between FY 1998-2002 to provide funds for the school breakfast
program.

HOUSE
Passed (429-1) H.R. 858 - Quincy Library Group Forest Recovery and
Economic Stability Act of 1997.
[SAP sent 7/9: "Administration would support if amendment to uphold
environmental laws and to clarify appropriations would be available"]

While under consideration, the House took the following action on H.R. 858:
o       Passed (voice vote) Miller/Young substitute amendment that
addressed the AdministrationD,s concerns listed in the SAP.

At press time, the House took the following action on H.R. 1775 -
Intelligence Authorization Act for FY 1998 (final vote is possible late
tonight) :
 [SAP sent 7/9: Administration supports House passage, and will work in
conference to make certain changes]
o        Rejected (142-289) Sanders amendment which would have reduced the
billD,s total authorization by 5%.
o        Rejected (192-237) Conyers amendment which would have
declassified the total amount appropriated for U.S. intelligence
activities.
