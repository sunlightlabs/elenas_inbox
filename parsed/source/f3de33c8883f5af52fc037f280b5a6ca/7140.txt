MEMORANDUM FOR THE PRESIDENT

FROM:          JUDITH A. WINSTON

THROUGH: ERSKINE BOWLES
         SYLVIA MATHEWS

DATE:          APRIL 17,1998

SUBJECT:       PRESIDENT'S INITIATIVE ON RACE WEEKLY REPORT
               APRIL 11 - APRIL 17



ADVISORY BOARD ACTIVITIES

        ESPN Meeting on Race and Sports_ On April 14, Advisory Board members
        Governor William Winter, Reverend Suzan Johnson Cook, Robert Thomas,
        Consultant Laura Harris and I attended your conversation on race sponsored by
        ESPN. The feedback we have received about the event has been positive, and
        I believe the conversation was successful in moving the dialogue on race forward
        and expanding our constituency.

        Thirtieth Anniversary ofthe Fair Housing Act. On April 23, 1998, the U.S.
        Department of Housing and Urban Development is conducting a public meeting and
        roundtable discussion in Newark, New Jersey, with representatives from fair housing and
        community organizations. The purpose of the meeting is to commemorate the 30th
        anniversary of the Fair Housing Act and discuss race and housing issues in New Jersey.
        Acting Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights Bill Lann Lee and HUD Assistant
        Secretary for Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity Eva Plaza are also participating in this
        event.

        Dillard University. On April 15, Governor Winter gave a luncheon address at the Ninth
        Annual Black-Jewish Relations Conference at Dillard University in New Orleans. The
        audience consisted of more than 100 students, faculty, and community leaders. Also
        participating in the conference were Congressman John Lewis and Reverend Walter
        Fauntroy.

     California State University, Fullerton. On April 16, Angela Oh spoke about the

OFFICE OF THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

     NAFEO Town Hall Meeting! National Neighborhood Coalition Information Forum.
      I spoke to both organizations about the Initiative. The remarks were very well received.

     Strategy Meeting on Indian Country. On April 17, members of your staff and the
     Initiative staff met to outline a strategy for further engaging Indian Country in the
     Initiative. We will be developing several options for discussion next week.

     Statewide Days of Dialogue. Currently 16 Governors and over 100 YWCA's are
     participating in our Statewide Days of Dialogue outreach effort. We are identifYing
     strong anchor events that will be the focus of Board and Cabinet participation as well as
     press attention.

     Race Relations in the Heartland. On April 14, the National Voices Coalition and
     HUD held a Regional Symposium in Kansas City, MO on "Race Relations in the
     Heartland," as "a response to President Clinton's year long initiative on race."
     Approximately 300 people from four states-- Missouri, Kansas, Nebraska, and
     lowa--attended. The Symposium was held in April to coincide with the 30th anniversary
     ofthe assassination of Martin Luther King, Jr. and the passage of the National Fair
     Housing Act.

     Meeting with the Eisenhower Foundation. We met with leaders of the Eisenhower
     Foundation to discuss their report entitled "The Millennium Breach: Richer, Poorer, and
     Racially Apart," which is a thirty-year update of the Kerner Commission's report. We
     discussed briefly the Foundation's findings and its recommendations concerning what
     works to overcome racial disparities in America. The report includes examples of what
     works, similar to our promising practices, and we agreed to explore how we might work
     together in this area.

     in Wisconsin have initiated on-site farm credit services for farmers on the Red
     Cliff and Bad River Tribes reservations to help them obtain credit to begin,
     expand, and operate farms and livestock operations.
